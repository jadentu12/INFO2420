Website Critiques

Questions:

  1. What was your first impression of this site?
  2. How does this site organize the flow of information?
  3. Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?
  4. Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?
  5. How long did it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?
  6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?
  7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?
  8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?
  9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?
  10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

Website 1: Trader Joe's

  1. The website's theme looks very cordial and easy to navigate. It seems to have the theme of a "market" which is fitting.
  2. You obviously have to scroll down but there are interactable buttons and images that where you can find more information
  3. It makes complete sense to me, its just a website for groceries and other food items and recipes. I can easily find any information I want.
  4. Enhances! There are many fonts, some look like handrwriting which makes it feel more personal to me. The images are also very helpful and make it more interesting.
  5. It was very fast, didn't seem to have an issue.
  6. Yes, it all seems to fit together and the text is easy to read.
  7. There was a navigation bar in the top left and it was very simple to read, not too much overwhelming information.
  8. Each page had a unique theme based on the type of page that it was, so it was nice to look at
  9. The text and images only fitted to a small window on my device when I made it extremely small, otherwise it didn't change much.
  10. It had featured recipes, like something that you would see in a restaurant

Website 2: Pitchfork

  1. There was a lot of information to see, but it looked slick and easy to read.
  2. It has a grid structure with articles in each square
  3. It makes sense to me, again, there is a lot of information which is expected but it is still readable.
  4. It enhances it because some of the images are moving and it is a cool feature to add, it makes it feel alive.
  5. It also loaded instantly, no issues. I also have a very good connection on my device.
  6. The font looked cool and it was just plain black with a large grey bar for the header. This is why I thought it looked slick.
  7. This site also had a navigation bar which was even more simple than the last. It containted highlights.
  8. The grey header bar on the top stayed, which made me feel as if I was still on the same website, which is something I can appreciate.
  9. The website neatly folded into a list in contrast to a grid system when I made the window small. Very nice to see.
  10. It was very clear on what articles were most important or trending, be using size and colors and tricks with images.

Website 3: Jamilin

  1. It was... very interesting to say the least. There are a lot of pictueres of the lady.
  2. Despite the weird theme, all the information was still organized into tables and was easy to read.
  3. It made sense. She offered different types of services and they were split respectively.
  4. There were 6 pictures of the same woman on the home page, maybe a little less would be nice but the colors and fonts looked good
  5. This site loaded fairly quickly, I was expecting it to load slower because I had no idea what it acutally was.
  6. Everything still seemed reasonable, it had sort of a fun feel to it because of the many colors.
  7. There was a horizontal navigation bar and it seemes easy to use, and very straight forward.
  8. The main site didnt seems to have a main color scheme, and the other linked sites just had "whatever" as the theme.
  9. The site did not react at all to the size of the window, regardless of what it was.
  10. Again, there were so many pictures of the lady! But if she wanted to associate her face with her brand, then I respect it I guess.
